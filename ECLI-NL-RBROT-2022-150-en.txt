3.1.

4.
The assessment in the incident
4.1.Because the woman and the man in Iran have married, the first question to be answered is whether the Dutch court has jurisdiction.This question must be answered on the basis of the Marriage Control Sters' Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/1103) because the claim was lodged after January 29, 2019.Pursuant to Article 5, paragraph 1 of the Marriage Convention Regulation, the Dutch court is competent because the request for divorce (pursuant to Article 3, paragraph 1, under a, first line), of Regulation (EC) 2201/2003, "Brussels-II BIS"))was brought to the Dutch court.The Dutch court therefore has jurisdiction.
4.2.
The second question to be answered is which judge is relatively competent.The woman bases her claim on the covenant.The parties have agreed that disputes that arise as a result of the covenant will be submitted exclusively to the competent court in Amsterdam.The present dispute is a dispute as a result of the covenant, so the Amsterdam District Court is exclusively authorized to take cognizance of the dispute.The parties stated at the hearing to agree to referring the case to the Amsterdam District Court.
4.3.
In view of the foregoing, the court will declare itself unauthorized and the case, pursuant to the provisions of Article 110, paragraph 2, of the Code of Civil Procedure, in the state in which it is located to the Amsterdam District Court, Trade sector.
4.4.
In view of the relationship between the parties, the costs between them will be compensated, in the sense that each party bears its own costs.
